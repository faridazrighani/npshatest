Pipe-flow friction analysis

Moody Chart Research Workbench

Interactive Darcy-Weisbach evaluation for incompressible single-phase liquid flow in circular pipes. The workflow follows the classical engineering sequence: velocity -> Reynolds number -> relative roughness -> friction factor -> pressure drop or head loss, now extended to Hydraulic Institute style system-head evaluation.

Scope and conventions

Darcy friction factor, SI units, steady fully developed internal liquid flow, circular pipes.

Laminar flow uses the exact relation f = 64 / Re; turbulent flow is solved with Colebrook-White; the transitional band is flagged and estimated with Churchill. Supply pressure, destination pressure, elevation, and minor losses extend the liquid-line calculation to Hydraulic Institute style system-head service.

Calculation Method

The implementation follows the conventional Darcy-Weisbach and Colebrook framework used in mechanical and hydraulic engineering.

1. Mean velocity

Area = pi D^2 / 4
V = Q / Area = 4Q / (pi D^2)

The mean bulk velocity is determined from the volumetric flow rate and internal pipe diameter.

2. Reynolds number

Re = rho V D / mu = V D / nu

This parameter determines whether the flow is laminar, transitional, or turbulent.

3. Relative roughness

epsilon / D

The non-dimensional wall roughness locates the operating point among the roughness families of the Moody chart.

4. Friction factor

Laminar:      f = 64 / Re
Transitional: Churchill correlation (flagged)
Turbulent:    Colebrook-White solved iteratively

The turbulent solver uses a Swamee-Jain explicit estimate as the initial guess, then refines the Darcy friction factor by fixed-point iteration.

5. Major pressure drop

DeltaP = f (L / D) (rho V^2 / 2)

This relation yields the distributed pressure loss caused by wall friction along the specified length.

6. Major head loss

hf = f (L / D) (V^2 / 2g)

The head-loss form is often preferred in pump, piping-network, and hydraulic-grade-line calculations.

7. Minor losses

DeltaP_minor = SigmaK (rho V^2 / 2)
hm = SigmaK (V^2 / 2g)

Aggregate local losses are assembled from SigmaK = sum(n_i K_i) + K_additional, covering fittings, valves, entrances, exits, and comparable disturbances.

8. Static head (HI form)

Delta h_stat = Delta z + (p_destination - p_supply) / (rho g)

This is the Hydraulic Institute form of static head for systems whose supply and destination boundaries have negligible velocity.

9. Total system head (HI system curve)

H_system = Delta h_stat + ((fL / D) + SigmaK) (V^2 / 2g)

This is the total head required by the system at a given flow rate. The application evaluates it at the operating point and also over a flow range to plot the system curve.

Assumptions and engineering notes

  • The current tool is intended for incompressible, Newtonian, non-gaseous, single-phase liquid flow in circular pipes.
  • The chart and formulas use the Darcy friction factor, not the Fanning friction factor.
  • The transitional interval is inherently uncertain; the tool therefore reports it explicitly rather than presenting false precision.
  • Absolute roughness values for commercial pipes are approximate design values; field-aged surfaces may deviate substantially.
  • The HI-style system curve assumes negligible velocities at the supply and destination boundaries unless those velocity-pressure terms are added separately.
  • Boundary pressures are treated as absolute liquid-service references only; no gas compressibility or two-phase model is included.
